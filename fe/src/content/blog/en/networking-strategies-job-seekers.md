# Networking Strategies for Job Seekers

Up to 80% of jobs are filled through networking, yet most job seekers spend the majority of their time submitting online applications. Building genuine professional relationships is the single most effective way to accelerate your job search.

## 1. Start with Your Existing Network

You already know more people than you think:

- **Former colleagues** — people who've seen your work firsthand
- **University alumni** — shared background creates instant rapport
- **Friends and family** — they may know someone in your target industry
- **Former managers** — often happy to make introductions

> "Don't ask for a job. Ask for advice. People love giving advice, and it often leads to opportunities."

Make a list of everyone you know and map them to your target companies or industries.

## 2. Leverage LinkedIn Effectively

LinkedIn is more than a digital resume — it's a networking engine:

- **Optimize your headline** — state what you do and what you're looking for
- **Engage with content** — comment thoughtfully on posts from people at target companies
- **Send personalized connection requests** — mention why you want to connect
- **Share your own insights** — publish posts about your field to build visibility

Avoid generic messages like "I'd like to add you to my network." Instead, reference something specific about the person's work.

## 3. Attend Industry Events

In-person and virtual events create opportunities that cold outreach can't:

- **Conferences and meetups** — find them on Meetup, Eventbrite, or industry Slack groups
- **Hackathons** — showcase your skills while meeting potential colleagues
- **Webinars and workshops** — lower commitment, still valuable for connections
- **Company open houses** — some companies host events specifically for potential candidates

Prepare a brief introduction that explains who you are and what you're interested in — not a rehearsed elevator pitch, but a natural conversation starter.

## 4. Offer Value Before Asking

Networking is a two-way street. Before asking for help:

- **Share useful articles or resources** relevant to their work
- **Make introductions** between people in your network who could benefit from knowing each other
- **Congratulate achievements** — promotions, published articles, product launches
- **Offer your expertise** — even a quick code review or design feedback builds goodwill

People remember those who helped them without expecting anything in return.

## 5. Follow Up Consistently

The real value of networking comes from sustained relationships:

- **Send a follow-up message** within 48 hours of meeting someone
- **Set reminders** to check in every few weeks
- **Share updates** about your job search progress
- **Thank people** who make introductions, regardless of the outcome

Keep track of every conversation, introduction, and follow-up. A disorganized approach leads to missed connections and forgotten promises.
