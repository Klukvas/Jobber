# How to Organize Your Job Search

Looking for a job can feel overwhelming, especially when you're juggling multiple applications at different stages. The key to success is treating your job search like a project — with clear processes, tracking, and regular reviews.

## 1. Create a Central Tracking System

Stop relying on memory or scattered sticky notes. Use a dedicated tool (like Jobber!) to track every application in one place. For each application, record:

- **Company name and position**
- **Date applied**
- **Current stage** (applied, phone screen, interview, offer)
- **Key contacts** and their roles
- **Follow-up dates**

## 2. Set Daily and Weekly Goals

Without structure, it's easy to lose momentum. Set targets like:

- Apply to 5 positions per week
- Send 3 follow-up emails per week
- Attend 1 networking event per month

## 3. Tailor Each Application

Generic applications get generic results. For each position:

- Customize your resume to highlight relevant experience
- Write a targeted cover letter
- Research the company culture and values
- Prepare specific questions for interviews

## 4. Track Your Results

After a few weeks, review your analytics:

- Which job sources yield the most interviews?
- What's your response rate?
- How long does each stage typically take?

This data helps you focus your efforts on what actually works.

## 5. Stay Consistent

The job search is a marathon, not a sprint. Block dedicated time each day for applications, follow-ups, and preparation. Consistency beats intensity every time.
