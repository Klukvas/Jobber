# Why You Should Track Every Job Application (And How to Do It)

Most job seekers apply to dozens of roles, then lose track of where they stand. They forget which resume version they sent, miss follow-up windows, and can't remember what they said in a cover letter when an interview call comes two weeks later.

Tracking fixes all of this.

## 1. The Chaos Without a System

Here's what untracked job searching looks like:

- You apply, then forget the company name by the time they call
- You send a tailored resume to one company - but which one?
- You don't follow up because you can't find the email thread
- You have no idea if LinkedIn, Indeed, or referrals are working better
- You accidentally apply to the same job twice

This isn't a discipline problem - it's a systems problem. A simple tracker solves it.

## 2. What to Track for Each Application

At minimum, record these fields for every application:

| Field | Why it matters |
|---|---|
| Company name | Obvious - you'll forget |
| Job title | You're applying to multiple roles |
| Date applied | For follow-up timing |
| Source | LinkedIn, Indeed, referral, company site |
| Status | Applied, screening, interview, offer, rejected |
| Resume version | Know what worked |
| Notes | Interview feedback, recruiter names |

The more data you capture, the more patterns you can spot.

## 3. The Kanban Method for Job Searching

Visualizing your pipeline as a Kanban board is one of the most effective ways to manage a job search.

Columns typically look like:

1. **Wishlist** - Companies you want to apply to
2. **Applied** - Sent the application
3. **Screening** - Phone or email contact
4. **Interviewing** - Active interview process
5. **Offer** - Negotiating or accepting
6. **Rejected / Closed** - For reference

Moving cards across columns gives you a clear visual of where your energy is going and where you're getting stuck.

## 4. Using Analytics to Improve Your Search

Once you have a few weeks of data, patterns emerge:

- **Response rate by source** - Are referrals getting 3x more responses than cold applications?
- **Time in stage** - Are you consistently stalling at the technical interview stage?
- **Resume performance** - Is version A of your resume outperforming version B?

This data lets you make real decisions: double down on what's working, fix what's not.

## 5. Tool Suggestions

You don't need an expensive tool to start. Options range from simple to powerful:

**Simple**: Google Sheets or Notion with a custom template
**Dedicated**: Jobber, Huntr, Teal - purpose-built for job search tracking
**Integrated**: Tools that combine tracking with resume management and analytics

The best tool is the one you'll actually use consistently. Start simple and upgrade when your search grows.
