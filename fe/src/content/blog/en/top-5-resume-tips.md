# Top 5 Resume Tips for 2026

Your resume is often the first impression you make on a potential employer. In a competitive job market, every detail matters. Here are five proven tips to make your resume stand out.

## 1. Lead with Impact, Not Duties

Recruiters don't want to read a list of responsibilities — they want to see results. Instead of:

> "Responsible for managing a team of developers"

Write:

> "Led a team of 8 developers, delivering 3 major features that increased user engagement by 40%"

Use numbers whenever possible. They make your achievements concrete and memorable.

## 2. Optimize for ATS Systems

Most companies use Applicant Tracking Systems (ATS) to filter resumes. To get past them:

- Use standard section headings (Experience, Education, Skills)
- Include keywords from the job description
- Avoid tables, columns, and complex formatting
- Save as PDF unless otherwise specified

## 3. Keep It Concise

For most professionals, a one-page resume is ideal. Two pages are acceptable for senior roles with 10+ years of experience. Cut anything that doesn't directly support the role you're applying for.

## 4. Tailor for Each Application

One-size-fits-all resumes don't work. For each application:

- Mirror the language from the job description
- Highlight the most relevant experience first
- Adjust your summary to match the role
- Remove irrelevant skills or experience

Using a tool like Jobber, you can track which resume version you used for each application and compare their effectiveness.

## 5. Proofread Relentlessly

Typos and grammatical errors signal carelessness. Before submitting:

- Read your resume aloud
- Use a spell-checker
- Ask a friend to review it
- Check formatting consistency
